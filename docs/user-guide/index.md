# User Guide

Welcome to Centabit - a budgeting app that helps you stay on track with your finances!

## What is Centabit?

Centabit is a modern budgeting application designed to help you:
- **Track spending** against your budgets in real-time
- **Understand your financial health** with the Budget Adherence Ratio (BAR) metric
- **Manage multiple budgets** for different time periods and purposes
- **Work offline** - all your data is stored locally on your device

## Quick Start Guide

Once you have Centabit installed, here's how to get started:

### 1. Create Your First Budget

1. Open the app and navigate to the **Budgets** tab
2. Tap the **"+"** button to create a new budget
3. Fill in budget details:
   - **Name**: e.g., "Monthly Groceries"
   - **Amount**: Total budget amount
   - **Start Date**: When the budget period begins
   - **End Date**: When the budget period ends

4. **Set Category Allocations**:
   - Assign portions of your budget to different spending categories
   - Example: $200 to Groceries, $100 to Restaurants
   - Allocations help you track spending by category

See detailed instructions in [Creating Budgets](./creating-budgets.md).

### 2. Add Transactions

1. Go to the **Transactions** tab
2. Tap the **"+"** button
3. Enter transaction details:
   - **Amount**: How much you spent
   - **Description**: What you bought
   - **Category**: Select from your categories
   - **Date**: When the transaction occurred
   - **Budget** (optional): Assign to a specific budget

Learn more in [Tracking Transactions](./tracking-transactions.md).

### 3. Monitor Your BAR

Check the **Dashboard** to see your Budget Adherence Ratio:

- **Green progress bar**: You're on track or ahead
- **Yellow/Orange bar**: Getting close to overspending
- **Red bar**: Overspending alert

The dashboard shows:
- Current BAR value
- Days remaining in budget period
- Amount spent vs. budget total
- Spending by category

### 4. Analyze Spending Patterns

Visit **Budget Details** to see:
- **Pie chart**: Spending by category allocation
- **Bar chart**: Actual spending vs. planned allocations
- **Transaction list**: All transactions for that budget
- **BAR trend**: How your ratio changes over time

## Tips for Success

### 1. Set Realistic Budgets
Start with actual spending data from your bank statements. Don't set budgets that are too restrictive - you'll get discouraged.

### 2. Track Regularly
Add transactions daily or weekly. The more current your data, the more useful your BAR metric becomes.

### 3. Use Categories Effectively
Assign every transaction to a category. This helps you understand spending patterns and identify areas to cut back.

### 4. Review Your BAR Weekly
Check in on your Budget Adherence Ratio at least once a week. Adjust spending if you notice BAR creeping above 1.0.

### 5. Adjust Allocations as Needed
If you consistently overspend in one category and underspend in another, adjust your allocations to match reality.
