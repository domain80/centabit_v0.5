# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, friendly, and harassment-free environment for everyone who contributes to or participates in the Centabit community. This applies to all community spaces, including GitHub discussions, issues, pull requests, and any other communication channels.

We value the participation of every member of our community and want everyone to have an enjoyable and fulfilling experience. All participants are expected to show respect and courtesy to others.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat everyone with respect. Value differing viewpoints and experiences.
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together towards common goals. Offer and accept constructive feedback gracefully.
- **Be Professional**: Use welcoming and inclusive language. Focus on what is best for the community and project.
- **Be Patient**: Understand that people have different skill levels and time commitments. Be patient with newcomers.
- **Be Open-Minded**: Welcome diverse perspectives and ideas. Be willing to learn from others.
- **Give Credit**: Acknowledge the contributions and work of others. Attribution matters.
- **Be Supportive**: Help others learn and grow. Share knowledge generously.

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, race, religion, or other personal characteristics
- Inappropriate use of sexual language or imagery
- Personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Sustained disruption of discussions or other community activities
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to:
- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct
- Ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Centabit community spaces, including:
- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Code comments and documentation
- Project communication channels
- Events or meetups (virtual or in-person) related to Centabit

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces, such as using an official project email address or social media account.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have concerns about potential violations of this Code of Conduct, please report it by:

1. **Opening a private issue**: Contact project maintainers directly through GitHub
2. **Email**: [Add contact email if available]

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. We are committed to treating all reports seriously and fairly.

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:
- Your contact information (so we can follow up)
- Names of individuals involved (if comfortable sharing)
- Description of what happened
- Date and time of the incident
- Any relevant screenshots or documentation
- Whether the situation is ongoing

### Response

Project maintainers will:
1. Acknowledge receipt of your report within 48 hours
2. Review the incident and gather necessary information
3. Determine appropriate action based on the severity and context
4. Communicate the outcome to the reporter (when appropriate)

### Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

**First Offense** (Minor):
- Private written warning from maintainers
- Request to apologize or correct behavior
- Temporary restriction from participation

**Second Offense** (Moderate):
- Public warning
- Temporary ban from community spaces (1-4 weeks)
- Required reflection period before returning

**Serious or Repeated Offenses**:
- Permanent ban from all community spaces
- Removal of all contributions (in severe cases)

The severity of the response will match the severity of the violation, and maintainers may use their judgment to determine appropriate action.

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was unjust, you may appeal by:
- Contacting a different maintainer privately
- Providing additional context or information
- Explaining why you believe the action was inappropriate

Appeals will be reviewed within 7 days of receipt.

## Our Values

### Inclusivity

We welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities. This includes, but is not limited to, members of any race, ethnicity, culture, national origin, color, immigration status, social and economic class, educational level, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, age, size, family status, political belief, religion, and mental and physical ability.

### Diversity

A diverse community is a strong community. We actively seek and welcome diverse perspectives and contributions. Different viewpoints and experiences make our project better.

### Growth

We believe everyone is here to learn and grow. We support:
- Beginners learning to code or design
- Experienced developers trying new patterns
- Anyone asking questions or making mistakes
- Continuous improvement and experimentation

### Quality

We strive for excellence while being supportive. Constructive feedback helps everyone improve. When reviewing code or designs:
- Focus on the work, not the person
- Explain why something could be improved
- Suggest alternatives or resources
- Acknowledge what's done well

### Transparency

Project decisions and discussions should be public when possible. Transparency builds trust and allows everyone to participate in shaping the project's future.

This Code of Conduct is a living document. We may update it based on community feedback and evolving best practices. Significant changes will be announced to the community.
